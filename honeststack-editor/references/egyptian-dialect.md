# Egyptian Football Voice Reference (2026)

Source-backed research on contemporary Egyptian-Arabic dialect for football
short-form video. Written for an AI scriptwriter targeting 16-35 Egyptian fans on
TikTok / Instagram / YouTube Shorts, voiced by ElevenLabs `eleven_multilingual_v2`
through a Pharaoh-mascot host.

This is the **deepest** voice reference in the project. `tone-guide.md` is the
persona overview; `voice-cheatsheet.md` is the mechanics quick-ref; this file is
the dialect bible. Read it once end-to-end, then dip back in by section when
writing or polishing.

## 1. The Three Voice Icons — verified catchphrases

### Bassem Youssef — sarcastic flat delivery, mock-serious

Bassem's voice is **deadpan deflation**: state the absurd fact dryly, then a
one-clause sarcastic interjection. His philosophy: *"Sarcasm is a weapon."*

Authentic patterns (use one per script, max):

- **«أحا!»** — one-word disbelief. Use VERY sparingly (borders on profane).
- **«طب اشمعنى…؟»** ("So why exactly…?") — fake-innocent question exposing a contradiction.
- **«يا سيدي يا سيدي»** ("Yes sir, yes sir") — mock-deference before demolition.
- **«ماشي يا عم، ماشي»** ("Sure thing, buddy, sure") — agreement that means the opposite.
- **«هو إحنا في فيلم؟»** ("Are we in a movie?") — reality-looks-scripted disbelief.
- **«ده كلام؟»** ("Is this even talk?") — about a quote you find ridiculous.
- **«شُفنا الفيلم ده قبل كده»** ("We've seen this movie before") — predictable scandals.
- **«احنا اللي غلطانين»** ("It's us who are wrong") — fake self-blame.
- **«حلو، حلو أوي»** — flat tone = sarcastic praise. The flatness IS the joke.
- **«طيب وبعدين؟»** ("OK and then what?") — bored skepticism.

**The Bassem move**: fact deadpan, beat, one-clause deflation. Example:
> «المدرب قال إن الفريق لعب كويس جدًا. تمام. الفريق خسر ٤-٠.»

### Amr Adib — emotional crescendo, dramatic repetition

The **vocal eruption**: whisper-low → builds with repetition + drawn-out vowels →
snaps into direct address.

Authentic patterns:

- **«إيه ده؟!… إيه ده؟!»** — double repetition with rising pitch (his signature shock).
- **«تاني يا [اسم]، تاني!»** ("Again [name], again!") — verified from his viral *تاني يا شيكابالا* clip.
- **«يااااااا… يا جماعة»** — drawn-out yaa, then "guys" — locks you in for what's next.
- **«قُل لي… قُل لي إنت»** ("Tell me… YOU tell me") — confrontational direct address.
- **«مش معقول… مش معقول!»** — doubled for shock.
- **«أنا مش هخبيها عليك»** ("I'm not gonna hide it from you") — folksy frankness opener.
- **«والله العظيم»** ("By God almighty") — oath-style emphasis.
- **Drawn-out player names**: «صلااااااح», «مرمووووش». Letter elongation = emotional weight, and ElevenLabs v2 actually extends the vowel for repeated letters.
- **«انت فاهم؟ انت فاهم اللي بقوله؟»** — drilling into the viewer.

**The Adib move**: repeat the key word twice, drag one vowel, direct "you" address.

Real channels documented:

- **Marwan Serry / Erza3** (إرزع) — 2M+ YouTube, animated reaction-video energy
- **Mogzz / إياد المجي** (Eyad Elmogy) — 1.2M+, fast cuts, exaggerated facial reactions

Patterns to teach:

- **«ده الكلام»** / **«هو ده الكلام»** ("That's the talk") — when an opinion lands.

- **«قُلت لكم ولا مقلتلكوش؟»** — vindication after a prediction.
- **«ده اللي بحبه في الكورة»** — emotional reset.
- **«النهارده، يومه»** — when a player performs.
- **«جامد يا معلم»** — praise.
- **«مفيش غيره»** — declaring a clear argument winner.
- **«افتح ودانك كويس»** ("Open your ears wide") — about to drop a take.
- **«تعالى ناخد بالنا من حاجة»** — analytical pivot.
- **«ع البلاطة كده»** ("Straight up like this") — bluntness opener.
- **«بصراحة كده»** — sincere mode (Bassem-inflected).

## 4. Egyptian comedy / satire conventions

### Setup → Land (the Egyptian beat)

The dominant pattern: **calm setup → flat fact → one-clause deflation**. The
humor lives in the GAP between claim and reality. Audiences fill the gap.
**Over-explaining kills it.**

> «المدرب طلع يقول إن الفريق لعب كويس جدًا. تمام. الفريق خسر ٤-٠.»

### Devices that work

- **Mock-deference**: praise fake-respectfully to expose. «يا سيدي يا سيدي، الكابتن العبقري قرر…»
- **Deadpan hyperbole**: «ده لو لعب بطيخ كان كسب».
- **Mock-disbelief questions**: «هو إحنا في فيلم؟».
- **Adib doubled repetition**: «تاني؟! تاني يحصل ده؟!».
- **"We've seen this before" callback**: «شفنا الفيلم ده قبل كده».
- **Self-deprecation via "we"**: «احنا اللي غلطانين، احنا اللي بنحب الكورة دي».
- **Wordplay/homophones**: structural backbone of Egyptian jokes. Use a player's name as a punchline if it doubles for something funny.

### Affectionate roast vs mean mocking — the line

- **Affectionate** = punch at the situation, the team's pattern, with warmth. Markers: «يا حبيبي», «يا واد», «والله بحبه بس…», soft "but we love him" closer. Egyptians laugh **with** this.
- **Mean** = personal attack, mocking appearance / poverty / origin, sectarian/regional slurs. Avoid: «ابن…» parent constructions, «صعيدي» jokes (unless self-told), anything about a player's family.

**The rule: roast the play, not the person.** Egyptians punish cruelty fast.

### What lands vs feels dated

- **Lands**: short, surprising, one twist, ends on the punch word. Arabic word order helps — put the load-bearing word last.
- **Dated**: poetic phrasing, full-MSA commentary, motivational-poster cadence, stacked emojis.

## 5. ElevenLabs `eleven_multilingual_v2` for Egyptian Arabic

**No SSML phoneme tags for Arabic** — only alias-style pronunciation
replacements work for non-English.

### Diacritics (تشكيل) — the high-leverage ones

The model defaults to MSA pronunciation. Add diacritics where Cairene flips:

1. **ج** is **/g/** in Egyptian (not /j/). Check voice output — if a generic Arabic voice says "jamid", consider Latin-alias swap to "gamed" in a pronunciation dictionary.
2. **ق** is **glottal stop /ʔ/** in Cairene. Write the colloquial spelling: قال → **أال**, قلب → **ألب**, قال للي → **أال للي**. Keep ق only for borrowed MSA-flavored words.
3. **ث / ذ / ظ merge in Egyptian**: ث→ت or س, ذ→د or ز, ظ→ض or ز. Use Egyptian forms: ثلاثة → **تلاتة**, ذا → **ده**, ظرف → **ضرف**.

### Egyptian-specific verb morphology — spell it Egyptian

- **بـ prefix on present** (continuous/habitual): "I play" = **بَلعب** (NOT ألعب alone — that's MSA).
- **هـ prefix for future**: "I will play" = **هلعب** (NOT سألعب — too formal).
- **ـش negation suffix**: "I didn't play" = **مَلَعبتش** / **ما لعبتش**. Essential.
- **بِتاع / بتاعي** possessive: very Egyptian. «الجون بتاع صلاح».

### Punctuation effects (RTL Arabic)

- **Use Arabic comma «،»** (U+060C) for natural pacing pauses — NOT Latin `,`.
- **Question mark is Arabic ؟** (U+061F) — proper rising intonation.
- **Ellipsis …** = pause; great for Adib-style dramatic breaks.
- **Repeat letters extend the vowel**: «صلاااااح» actually drags. Use for shock.
- **Numerals**: default normalizer expands `2` → "اثنان" (MSA). Spell scores in Egyptian: «اتنين-واحد» NOT `2-1`.

### EMOJIS — STRIP FROM TTS INPUT

ElevenLabs' own docs warn that emojis "can confuse the AI" and may be read
aloud as text. **Never put emojis in `script_segments.text`** — that field
becomes BOTH on-screen caption AND TTS input.

Emojis are fine in:
- `hook` (display only, not narrated)
- `brief.cta` (display only)
- Outro card hashtags
- Cockpit metadata
- Social bios

But the spoken script text must be **emoji-free**.

### Suggested settings for the Pharaoh creator voice

- Stability ~0.40 (current — confirmed lower = more expressive)
- Similarity ~0.75 (current)
- Style ~0.30-0.35 (current)
- Keep IDENTICAL across the 4-daily-video batch for persona consistency.

## 6. Pitfalls / red flags — "not really Egyptian"

### MSA contamination (dead giveaways)

| ❌ MSA | ✅ Egyptian |
|---|---|
| هذا / هذه / هؤلاء | **ده / دي / دول** |
| ماذا | **إيه** |
| كيف | **إزاي** |
| لماذا | **ليه** |
| أين | **فين** |
| الآن | **دلوقتي** |
| جدًا (alone) | **أوي / قوي / فشخ / موت** |
| يلعب الآن | **بيلعب دلوقتي** |
| سيلعب | **هيلعب** |
| لا يلعب / لم يلعب | **مبيلعبش / ملعبش** |
| أريد | **عايز** |
| جيد | **كويس / حلو / جامد** |
| كثير | **كتير** |
| لكن (only) | **بس** (Egyptian preferred) |

### Levantine intrusions (common AI mistake)

- ❌ هيك → ✅ **كده**
- ❌ شو → ✅ **إيه**
- ❌ كيفك → ✅ **إزيك**
- ❌ هلق → ✅ **دلوقتي**
- ❌ منيح → ✅ **كويس**
- ❌ ليش → ✅ **ليه**
- ❌ بدي → ✅ **عايز**

### Khaleeji intrusions

- ❌ وايد → ✅ **أوي / قوي / فشخ**
- ❌ شلونك → ✅ **إزيك**
- ❌ هسة → ✅ **دلوقتي**
- ❌ يبه / يبا → ✅ **يا واد / يا عم / يا جدع**
- ❌ زين → ✅ **كويس / تمام**

### AI-generated mistakes to scrub

- **Overusing رائع / مذهل / للغاية** — MSA-academic. Humans say **جامد / فشخ / حلو أوي**.
- **Stacking adjectives** — Egyptian speech is verb-heavy. ❌ «هدف رائع جميل ساحق» → ✅ «ضربها فشخ» / «حطها ع البلاطة».
- **Starting sentences with «إنّ»** — pure MSA tic. Egyptians don't.
- **Long compound sentences with حيث / كما / إذ / بحيث** — academic. Break into short clauses.
- **Stock closings** like «ودمتم بخير» / «والسلام عليكم» / «إلى اللقاء» — too formal. Egyptian creators close with **«نتقابل بكره»** / **«خد بالك من نفسك»** / **«سلام»** or a hard cut.
- **Third-person rhetorical loops** «هل سجل صلاح هدفًا؟ نعم لقد سجل…» — academic essay cadence. Real Egyptians don't.

### Cringe / dated (avoid)

- **«ولا في الأحلام»** — 2018-meme dead.
- **«تمام التمام»** — written-vibe, not spoken.
- **«حلو حلو»** repeated — became meme, died.
- **«وحشتنا يا [team]»** about a bad team — 2020 joke, played out.
- **«اللي يعرف يعرف»** — TikTok cliché, overcooked.
- **«ع راسي / فوق راسي»** as non-sincere flex — sounds Boomer.

## 7. Time-zone humor for World Cup 2026 (USA/Canada/Mexico)

Cairo is UTC+2. Prime-time US matches = 22:00-06:00 Cairo. Egypt's group-stage:
**Belgium 22:00 Cairo, New Zealand 04:00, Iran 06:00**. The fan-base splits
between **all-nighters** and **morning-after catch-uppers** — that's the joke
surface.

### Authentic patterns to draw from

1. **«اللي نام، فاته العالم»** — frame for "you slept on it" videos.
2. **«صحيت الساعة ٤، عشان يا روحي يا روحي»** — self-mock-martyrdom.
3. **«إحنا اللي صاحيين، إنتوا اللي نايمين»** — split-the-audience hook.
4. **«ده مش ماتش، ده دوام»** — commitment joke.
5. **«النوم بقى لوكشري»** — June-long resigned tone.
6. **«صحيت لقيت الجون، ونمت تاني»** — humble flex.
7. **«قهوتي وأنا في الـ٤ صبحًا، عشانكم»** — host-as-sacrifice meme.
8. **«اللي بيشتغل بكره، يا ربي يحفظه»** — solidarity joke.
9. **«نمتوا؟ طب احنا هنحكيلكم»** ⭐ — HonestStack tagline material. Positions the brand as "I stayed up so you don't have to."
10. **«صلاتك الفجر النهارده هيكون عندك عذر»** — culturally affectionate dawn-prayer overlap.
11. **«٦ صبح كأس العالم، ٧ صبح المدير بيكلمك»** — work-life joke.
12. **«اللي نام في الشوط الأول، يا حرام»** — affectionate jab.

### HonestStack positioning

> **«إحنا صحينا عشانك، إنت تابعنا وريّح»**

We stayed up for you, you just follow us and rest. This frames the 4-daily-roundup
model perfectly: the Pharaoh is the **eternal awake**, the audience is the
**forgiven sleeper**. Slightly conspiratorial "you and me both know we should be
asleep" tone — authentically Egyptian, fits late-night ahwa energy, sustainable
through the whole tournament.

## Caveats from the research

1. Specific creator catchphrases (Serry, Mogzz, Nso7y, Yehia) are derived from
   channel style + aggregated football-creator patterns — text transcripts are
   scarce. **Recommend watching 5-10 actual Erza3 / Nso7y videos to validate

2. TikTok creator culture moves faster than indexed web — sample current
   trending phrases from live TikTok directly each ~3 months for refreshes.

   calibration scripts before assuming numbers are optimal.
